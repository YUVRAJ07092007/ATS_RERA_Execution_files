## A. Binding HARERA background (not outside scope)

Your reply treats my queries on pre-EMI, possession, construction status, and escrow account (Points 12 and 13) as unrelated to the plan-revision exercise. This position is **legally untenable**.

The HARERA Order dated **18.04.2024** in Complaint No. **4463 of 2021** is a final authority order against ATS Realworth Pvt. Ltd. in respect of my unit. The **certified copy** further records that:

- rectification under Section 39 was **allowed** on 08.08.2024;
- Rs. 7,20,000/- is the booking amount and the balance was routed through PNB Housing Finance Ltd.;
- the respondent is bound to pay pre-EMI till offer of possession on the loan amount received from the financial institution;
- the respondent must disclose construction status, possession date, and escrow account details;
- the respondent’s defence was **struck off** for failure to file written reply.

These obligations remain **fully binding** on the existing and proposed developer/promoter. Any revised plan, change of developer, or extended possession timeline cannot be used to override, dilute, or postpone compliance with the HARERA order.

## C. Specific objections arising from your own comparative chart

From the comparative chart enclosed by you, the following changes are specifically noted and objected to:

1. **Project intensity increased:** main units proposed increased from 522 to 734.  
2. **Parking reduced:** ECS reduced from 999 to 957 despite higher unit count.  
3. **Green/open area reduced:** from 14345.86 sqm to 10934.25 sqm.  
4. **Tower 03 materially altered:** additional podium levels, increased height, revised floor stack (S1+S2+30), revised staircase/lift configuration, and changed balcony design.  
5. **Possession deferred to 30.06.2031** for Phase-I, contrary to buyer agreement date of 30.06.2023 and HARERA order.

These changes are **not** mere “improvements” as described in your reply. They affect density, parking, common areas, vertical development, and possession timeline, and therefore require lawful individual notice, complete disclosure, and informed consent.
